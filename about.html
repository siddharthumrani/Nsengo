About NSENGO

A world of opportunities for smallholder farmers

In the local language of the Republic of Congo, NSENGO means “hoe.” This multi-purpose farming tool is indispensable for farmers as they prepare their fields for planting and remove weeds. It’s the symbol of farming in Africa. Just as the hoe helps plants grow strong and healthy, NSENGO aims at building an ecosystem around about 550 million smallholder farmers. They are one of Africa’s greatest strengths as they produce 80% of the food consumed across the continent.

Vision

Our vision is to contribute to the green revolution in Africa by helping smallholder farmers feed their continent and live out of poverty.

Mission

We are a social enterprise connecting smallholder farmers to a world of opportunity, so they can improve their productivity and incomes.

Agriculture being the heart of the solution to Africa’s poverty, we commit to contributing to Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) #1 (Poverty) and #2 (Hunger). We will help feed on the continent 1.5 billion people by 2030 and 2 billion by 2050.

What We Do

Digital financial inclusion to help farmers borrow at reasonable costs.

Global best farming practices to increase crop yields.

High-quality, timely weather data to improve planting.

Artificial intelligence access to protect crops and increase yields.

Effective tools and services from agricultural partners to control costs.

Microinsurance to insure crops against unforeseen crop damage and risks.
