About NSENGO

In the language of the Republic of Congo, “nsengo” means “hoe.” This multi-purpose farming tool is indispensable for farmers as they prepare their fields for planting and remove weeds. It’s the symbol of farming in Africa. Just as the hoe helps plants grow strong and healthy, NSENGO aims to help smallholder farmers grow their businesses.

Contributors

Smallholder farmers are one of Africa’s greatest strengths. They produce 80% of the food consumed across the continent!

Half a billion smallholder farmers operate farms of 2 hectares or less. Yet, 240 million Africans are still without food, and the number will climb to 320 million by 2025.

Smallholder farmers struggle against the persistent cycle of hunger and poverty because of low crop yields and unpredictable profits.

These smallholder farmers are a majorly underserved and underbanked population.

Because they struggle against high interest rates and collateral requirements, smallholder farmers experience insufficient access to credit.
